The Berenson Library at Villa I Tatti

When Bernard Berenson bequeathed his villa and collections to Harvard University in 1959, the library held over 50,000 volumes and 170,000 photographs. Under Harvard's stewardship, the collections have grown to nearly 200,000 volumes, 300,000 photographs, and extensive archival holdings — making it one of the world's preeminent research libraries for Italian Renaissance studies.

The Digital Collections Portal provides unified search and curated access to these holdings, bringing a century of art-historical scholarship to researchers worldwide.
